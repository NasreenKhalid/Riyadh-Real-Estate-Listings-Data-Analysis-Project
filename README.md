📈  Finding 1 — Strong overall market growth
Source: Price Growth KPI + Price Trend by Year chart
Riyadh property prices grew by +20.47% from 2021 to 2024, with the sharpest jump happening between 2022 and 2023. Notably, prices actually dipped slightly in 2022 before recovering strongly — suggesting a brief post-pandemic correction before Vision 2030 investment accelerated demand.

🏘️ Finding 2 — Al Olaya commands a massive price premium
Source: Top 10 Districts by AVG Price chart
Al Olaya is the most expensive district at SAR 1.59M average, more than 2× the price of bottom-ranked districts like Al Maather and Al Rajhi. Central zone districts (blue bars) dominate the top 3, while North zone districts (orange) cluster in the mid-range.

🏠 Finding 3 — Apartments dominate supply, Villas dominate value
Source: Property Type Distribution donut + Price by Property Type bar
Apartments make up 38.9% of all listings — the largest share of any type. But Villas command the highest average price at SAR 2.2M — nearly 9× the price of a Studio (SAR 251K). The market is volume-driven by affordable units but value-driven by luxury properties.

📐 Finding 4 — Area is the strongest price driver
Source: Price Scatter Plot by Property Type
The scatter plot shows a clear diagonal trend — as property area increases, price increases proportionally across all types. Studios and Apartments cluster bottom-left (small, affordable), Villas spread top-right (large, expensive). The separation between coloured clusters confirms that property type and size together explain most of the price variation.

⏱️ Finding 5 — Properties take ~90 days on average to sell
Source: AVG Days Listed KPI
The average listing stays on market for 90.6 days — roughly 3 months. This is a useful baseline: districts or property types with significantly fewer days indicate high demand / hot markets, while those above 90 days signal oversupply or pricing issues.
